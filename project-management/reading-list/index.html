Community Management Reading List

This page is a reading list of resources about project management, product development, and community management. It is a catch-all reading list for anything related to supporting the humans behind the Open Source projects. While there are many resources on these topics around the web, this reading list focuses specifically on an Open Source perspective. If you are familiar with the role of a developer advocate, there is some overlap between the responsibilities of that type of position.

Foundations 🔗

  • [Open Source Guides](https://opensource.guide/) - curated by GitHub

  • Covers a broad range of topics and concepts to allow you to focus in on specific areas

  • [Introduction to Open Source](https://www.digitalocean.com/community/tutorial_series/an-introduction-to-open-source) - Digital Ocean

  • [Frequently asked questions about open source](https://opensource.com/resources) - Opensource.com

Defining strategy 🔗

  • 6 steps to perfecting an Open Source product strategy - Michael DeHaan

  • A Free Guide for Setting Your Open Source Strategy - Linux Foundation

  • Producing Open Source Software - Karl Fogel (includes resources on almost all major topics)

  • Setting an Open Source Strategy - Linux Foundation

Managing in the open 🔗

  • How we organize GitHub issues: A simple styleguide for tagging - Zach Dunn (@zachdunn)

  • (video) [Have It Your Way: Maximizing Drive-Thru Contributions](https://archive.org/details/ato2017-drivethru) - VM Brasseur [@vmbrasseur](https://github.com/vmbrasseur)

Methodologies 🔗

  • Why (some) agile teams fail - Jen Krieger (@mrry550)

Additional readings 🔗

  • (book) [Forge Your Future with Open Source](https://fossforge.com/) - VM Brasseur [@vmbrasseur](https://github.com/vmbrasseur)

  • This is an excellent peer-reviewed book about building and growing FOSS projects and a community around the project. It is well worth the $20 USD cost.
